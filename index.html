Fjeldskred (Rock avalanche)

Large and extremely fast flow of boulders in steep terrain. A landslide is actually a current, but the term is commonly used and is included here as a technical term.

About

Concept

This project was created in collaboration with Dokk1, Aarhus, with the aim of raising awareness of the newly introduced formal Danish nomenclature for landslides, as established in a paper by Kristian Svennevig and Marie Keiding (2020), published in the Geological Journal. You can find the scientific article here.

The art installation employs a mix of techniques, combining sashiko-inspired embroidery with acrylic paints on canvas to depict a significant landslide site in Denmark, namely Møns Klint. It comprises 42 individual embroidery pieces, each representing a specific landslide term through a unique pattern drawn from the paper mentioned above. The terms span four categories: landslide movement, landslide types, activity, and landslide morphology.

The embroidery pieces are placed onto an oval-shaped canvas, together with acrylic paints, to create a natural landmark that also hints at the many natural processes connected to landslides.

Tribute to Landslides, created by Despina Ivanova and Zuzanna Krauzowicz, is an invitation to reconsider landslides long overlooked in Denmark, while celebrating the relation between human beings and the natural world.

Additionally, you can find here an interactive map showing all recorded landslides across Denmark, allowing you to explore their locations and distribution nationwide.
